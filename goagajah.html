Cave Entrance

The Holy Pond

Goa Gajah translates to Elephant Cave. The face carved into the wall of stone is supposed to ward off evil spirits. The interior of the cave is shaped like a captial T. If you turn left at the end of the tunnel you will find a shrine of Lord Ganesha. On the right you will find a shrine representing Lord Shiv and Shakti. The only source of light inside the cave comes from burning candles. Black soot lines the walls from the burning of incense. When you are finished exploring the cave, head to the ravine to take in the natural beauty of the landscape.

Goa Gajah is an active place of worship. Please respect the temple and it's customs. Do not take photos of anyone in active worship. A sarong must be worn to cover below the knee. They are available for rent at the entrance. Acces to Goa Gojah is restricted to anyone who is bleeding. This includes menstruating women and women who have given childbirth within the last 6 weeks and any person with an open wound.
