Bali is home to the most expensive coffee in the world

Kopi is the Indonesian word for coffee.

Luwak refers to an animal called a civet.

Kopi Luwak is recognized as a luxury coffee. It is rare and desirable. But why? Kopi Luwak is actually not a type of coffee, but rather a method of production.

This coffee is unique because it is harvested from the stool of the civet cat. Yes, you read that right. It is cat poop coffee.

Kopi Luwak @ Manik Abian

This adorable creature is a civet.

This cat-like mammal feeds on a variety of insects, seeds, and nuts, but it is best known for eating coffee cherries.

The civet's powerful sense of smell helps it identify and consume only the ripest coffee cherries. During the 24 to 36 hour process of digestion, the enzymes in the civet's digestive tract remove any fruit exterior and alter the chemical composition of the coffee beans in a way that makes them taste less acidic. Workers then collect the coffee beans from the civet's poop, thoroughly wash, dry, roast, ground, and brew them.

The result is a delicious cup of joe.

One cup of Luwak Coffee costs around 80,000 IDR. That is ~ $6 per cup!

It is difficult to find ethically sourced Kopi Luwak. Sometimes the beans come from wild civets. In most instances, however, civets are confined to small cages and victims of abuse. Many people worry about the treatment and health of civet cats. It is not uncommon for civets diets to be restricted to only coffee cherries. This can lead to an assortment of health problems.
