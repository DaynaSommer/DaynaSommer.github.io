Uluwatu Temple is a Balinese Hindu sea temple.

This ancient temple is believed to be one of Bali's spirtirual pillars.

Uluwatu Temple sits 70 metres above sea level.

It is renowed for it's magnificent location overlooking the ocean.

Entry into Uluwatu Temple is 15,000 IDR ~ $1 domestic and 30,000 IDR ~ $2 for foreigners.

Bali temples are common places of worship. All visitors should wear a sarong to show respect within any Balinese temple. A sarong is tied around the waist and is used to cover the legs below the knee. The sarong is included in the entrance fee.

While visiting Uluwatu Temple, do not miss the Kecak fire dance! Grab your tickets early because the show will sell out. Over 75 male dancers combine sound, fire, and costume to tell an elaborate story. The show starts at 6 PM in perfect harmony with the sunset. I recommend you arrive at 5 PM to claim a good seat. Tickets are 100,000 IDR ~ $6.50 per person.
